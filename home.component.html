About Us

The Art Institute of Chicago, founded in 1879, is one of the oldest and largest art museums in the United States. With a collection of nearly 300,000 works of art, it is an encyclopedic museum that covers thousands of years of human expression from around the world. Our permanent collection includes masterpieces such as Georges Seurat's A Sunday on La Grande Jatte, Pablo Picasso's The Old Guitarist, Edward Hopper's Nighthawks, and Grant Wood's American Gothic, as well as countless other treasures from ancient times to today.
